Diffie-Hellmann Key Exchange

Public-key protocols. Given A,B and eavesdropper C and assuming colour seperation is expensive:

  • A and B publically agree on a common colour P.
  • A and B choose some secret colour X and Y respectively.
  • A mixes P+X and sends to B
  • B mixes P+Y and sends to A
  • C intercepts P+x and P+Y
  • A and B then mix their personal secret colours to the received colors.
  • A's new mix = P+Y+X
  • B's new mix = P+X+Y

Therefore both arrive at the same colour. However C could not come up with P , X and Y from P+x and P+Y as they are inseperable. Thus C can't make P+X+Y and thus sing P+X+Y is now the secure private key for A and B.
